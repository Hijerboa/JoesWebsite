Virus Removal

We use tried and tested software to remove all sorts of malware and viruses from your computer. Our methods ensure that your personal files and data remain intact, while at the same time removing all traces of the virus/malware from your computer.

If your computer is currently infected with malware, there is a good chance that it is already spreading to other devices on your network, as well as to your friends and family in your address book. Other malicious programs will remain quiet on your computer, collecting your person information, as well as things like your credit card numbers, passwords, or personal data. In order to help defend you against these sort of threats, we offer Malwarebytes Premium subscriptions at a discounted rate, to best protect you from the makers of this malicious software

The most malicious form of malware comes in the form of ransomware. If you find your computer locked with a message telling you to call a number and pay a fee to unlock your files, then you have been infected with ransomware. To best protect yourself from this kind of malware, ensure that you take regular backups of your computer

Common Virus Symptoms:

  • Are you blocked out of your Windows programs or unable to access the Internet?
  • Does your computer frequently restart? or stop responding?
  • Do you click on a search result and get re-directed to a completely different page?
  • Do you have an "automatic" virus scan coming up on your computer telling you that you need to purchase a lifetime subscription to a software you've never downloaded?
  • Is your computer is acting funny?
  • Is your internet feeling very slow?

While many viruses can be removed by our software, some may be incredibly well rooted into your computer, meaning that a full Windows reinstallation (see below) may be necessary to ensure that the virus is fully removed.

Hardware Upgrades

Every day, new programs are being developed for our computers, and new programs are increasingly using more and more of our computers' resources. A computer that may once have been snappy years ago, often no will drag on even the most basic applications.

One of the best ways to stop your computing experience from dragging is to perform hardware upgrades on your computer. Two of the most effective, as well as cost effective, upgrades are adding more RAM and installing an SSD in your computer.

Adding More RAM:

Random Access Memory (RAM) is what your computer uses to access files which it is currently using. Since RAM is much faster than your Hard Drive, which stores the bulk of the information on your computer, your computer stores what it is currently using in RAM. However, if what you are trying to do on your computer requires more space than there is available RAM, the computer will be forced to keep this information on your hard drive, which is significantly slower. You will immediately feel when your computer does this. By adding more RAM, you will give your computer more space to store the files it is using, and noticeably speed up your computer.

Adding an SSD:

A traditional hard drive is made of spinning magnetic platters. While these platters spin at extremely high speeds, there is a maximum speed these platters can spin, resulting in a limit to how fast your data can be read. SSD's transfer data via electrons, which is significantly faster. While more expensive per GB than standard Hard Drive's, SSD's will increase the speed of your computing experience drastically. Your machine will boot much faster, often times in mere seconds. Your programs will launch so fast you won't even get a chance to get a cup of coffee. If you chose to upgrade your machine to an SSD, we can either clone your Hard Drive to the new SSD, getting you back up and running without missing a beat, or we can reinstall Windows from scratch (see above) to make your computer feel like new again.

No matter what you chose, we will ensure that your computer is up and running again as quickly as possible, and performing better than ever.
